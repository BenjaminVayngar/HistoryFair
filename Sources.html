Primary Sources



Cline, David P., director. John Carlos oral history interview conducted by David P. Cline in New York, New York, 2013 August 18. 2013. Library Of Congress, https://www.loc.gov/item/2015669202/?loclr=blogtea. Accessed 24 October 2024.


This source is mainly about Carlos’s life. This brings up some interesting points about his life experiences and helps add some background information. It proves our thesis by showing what Carlos did regardless of being discriminated against and how he proceeded to still do great things.


Davis, David. “Olympic Athletes Who Took a Stand.” Smithsonian Magazine, August 2008, https://www.smithsonianmag.com/articles/olympic-athletes-who-took-a-stand-593920. Accessed 14 October 2024.


The source is about Olympic athletes, Tommie Smith and John Carlos, who took a stand about things they did after the Olympics and personal opinions on how they felt about their actions. This helps this project by showing how these athletes impacted the African American communities by fighting for human rights for African Americans. The athletes wore black socks without shoes to symbolize African-American poverty. They also wore black gloves to express African-American strength and unity.


Maguire, Gregory. “In History: How Tommie Smith and John Carlos's protest at the 1968 Mexico City Olympics shook the world.” BBC, 15 October 2023, https://bbc.com/culture/article/20231011-in-history-how-tommie-smith-and-john-carloss-protest-at-the-1968-mexico-city-olympics-shook-the-world. This source is mainly about the 1968 Olympics, giving helpful background information and where the Black Power Salute originated from.

“1968 Olympics Black Power salute.” Wikipedia, 7 December 2024, https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1968_Olympics_Black_Power_salute. Accessed 11 December 2024.


During the medal ceremony in the Olympic Stadium, Mexico City, October 16, 1968, Tommie Smith and John Carlos, two African-American athletes, both raised a black-gloved fist during the playing of the U.S. national anthem and talked about how they felt proud about what they did because they were spreading awareness and that was were important to them.
